Welcome to Missouri Birding Guide

Missouri is home to a rich variety of native bird species, shaped by the state’s diverse landscapes and unique geology. From the limestone hills of the Ozarks to wetlands and river valleys carved by the Mississippi and Missouri Rivers, each region supports distinctive bird populations. This site explores Missouri’s native birds, their habits and behaviors, and the natural features that make the state an important habitat for both resident and migratory species.

Discover Missouri's Native Bird Species

Explore the incredible diversity of birds that call Missouri home, from forest songbirds to wetland waterfowl. Learn about the species that thrive across the state’s unique landscapes and what makes each one an important part of Missouri’s natural heritage.

Habitats and Seasonal Patterns

Discover how Missouri’s forests, prairies, wetlands, and river valleys shape where birds live and how they survive. Explore how changing seasons influence migration, nesting, feeding, and behavior throughout the year.

Why Native Birds Matter

Learn why native birds play a vital role in Missouri’s ecosystems, from controlling insects to pollinating plants and spreading seeds. Discover how protecting these species helps preserve the balance and beauty of the state’s natural environment.
