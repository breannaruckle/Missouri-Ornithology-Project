Understanding Where Birds Live and When They Migrate

Missouri’s varied geography creates a wide range of habitats that support native bird populations. Forests, prairies, wetlands, rivers, and urban green spaces each provide essential resources such as food, shelter, and nesting areas. These environments work together to support both year-round resident birds and seasonal migratory species that pass through the region.

Seasonal changes strongly influence bird behavior across the state. Migration patterns, breeding cycles, and feeding habits shift throughout the year, making Missouri an important location for observing a diverse range of bird species. During spring and fall, many birds use Missouri as a critical stopover point, allowing birdwatchers to see species that may not stay year-round.

By understanding how habitats and seasons affect bird activity, you can improve your chances of spotting different species and better appreciate the natural environments they depend on. Whether you are exploring forests, wetlands, or open prairies, each habitat offers unique opportunities to observe birds in their natural surroundings.

Report a Bird Sighting

Use this form to record a bird sighting and help track where different bird species are seen across Missouri. Submitting accurate observations helps build a better understanding of bird activity, migration patterns, and habitat use.

Try to include as much detail as possible, including the location, date, and number of birds observed. Clear and consistent data makes it easier to identify trends and contributes to a more complete picture of bird populations in your area.
